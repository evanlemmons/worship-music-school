# Worship Music School — Program Overview & Plan

**North Metro Church · Kennesaw, GA**
**Launching:** Sunday, September 6, 2026 · **Prepared:** July 2026

---

## In one sentence

A weekly discipleship-and-development group that forms young musicians into worshipers first and skilled, stage-ready band members second — growing them in Christ, in craft, and in the real-world skills of playing music together.

## Why this exists

Most churches can teach a teenager to play an instrument, and many can run a Bible study. Very few do both in the same room, on purpose, with people who have actually done it professionally. This group closes that gap. We want students to leave able to (1) articulate what worship really is and live like it, (2) understand the whole ecosystem of being a working musician — not just the notes, but the gear, the team, and the preparation — and (3) sit in with a band and hold their part on a real stage.

The heart of it is discipleship through music.

## The three-part weekly meeting

Every meeting is built on the same three sections, in this order. The order matters: we ground ourselves before we sharpen skills, and we sharpen skills before we play, so that playing together is the celebration the rest of the night points toward.

**1. Discipleship (~15 min).** A short, discussion-driven Bible study. For the first term the studies deliberately explore *worship as a whole life*, not just singing — because a musician who understands that will lead differently for the rest of their life. We read from the NIV, non-denominational and Christ-centered, and we spend real time getting to know each other.

**2. Musicianship (~20 min).** The non-obvious craft of being a musician: signal flow and gear, in-ear monitors, playing to a click, charts, rehearsing at home versus rehearsing as a band, the role of a music director, planning a set, and the production world (sound, lighting, lyrics, stage plots). These are the things nobody teaches you and everybody expects you to know.

**3. Playing together (~25 min).** We learn and work a song as a band — usually one song per one-to-two weeks depending on difficulty — and actually play it. The repertoire leans worship, with the occasional secular song mixed in for range and fun. Every song is chosen to reinforce that week's musicianship lesson wherever possible.

*Timing note:* the split above assumes a ~60-minute meeting. If we stretch to 90 minutes, expand to roughly 25 / 30 / 35. For a full 2 hours, the extra time is best spent on hands-on gear and a longer band run-through.

## Who it's for

Young musicians, middle + high school age (~12–18) — confirmed, all levels, no floor — at a range of experience levels, from students still learning their instrument to those ready for real repertoire. The curriculum is built with tiered parts so a beginner and an experienced player can be in the same band and both have something to play. *(Experience mix and group size are still assumptions to confirm — see the final section.)*

## The shape of the first two terms

We treat September through February as two connected terms with a natural break at Christmas.

**Fall term — "What Is Worship?" (Sept 6 – Dec 13, ~14 weeks).** Foundations. Discipleship builds a biblical theology of worship-as-life. Musicianship covers the ecosystem of playing — gear, signal flow, in-ears, click, charts, rehearsing. Repertoire starts with accessible four-chord worship songs and grows. The term ends in a **Showcase** the week before Christmas break, where the students play the songs they've built — for each other, for parents, or folded into a youth service.

**Winter term — "The Worshiping Life" (Jan 3 – Feb 21, ~8 weeks).** Deepening. Discipleship moves into the Psalms and the character of a worship leader. Musicianship moves into gear deep-dives, set planning, leading versus playing, and self-review. The band works toward playing a full, connected set rather than isolated songs.

## What students build toward (milestones)

The program is designed around visible milestones so students, parents, and leaders can all see progress:

1. **First song, start to finish** (by ~week 3–4) — the whole band plays one song together.
2. **Fall Showcase** (week of Dec 13) — the term's repertoire performed; the first "real" moment.
3. **A full connected set** (by end of Feb) — not isolated songs but a planned, flowing set with transitions.
4. **Serving on a real team** (ongoing/aspirational) — students who are ready begin shadowing or serving on a North Metro team.

## How we'll know it's working

We're watching for four things: students can *explain* what worship is in their own words; they show up to play *prepared* (charts learned, parts practiced at home); the band can hold a song together with a click and dynamics; and — the quiet one that matters most — they *want to be there*, and they're inviting friends.

## What we'd ask of the church

A consistent room with power and enough space for a small band setup; access to (or storage for) basic backline — drums, keys, a couple of amps/DIs, a small PA or monitor solution, and mics; a modest annual budget for consumables (cables, sticks, strings, batteries, in-ear tips) and any curriculum materials; and a clear point of contact on the staff side. A starter gear-and-budget list is in **"07 — Logistics, Safety & Open Questions."**
