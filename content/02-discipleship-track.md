# Discipleship Track — Bible Studies

**Translation:** NIV · **Posture:** non-denominational, Christ-centered, discussion-driven
**Format:** ~15 minutes each week — open together, read the passage, talk, close.

The organizing idea for the first two terms is that **worship is not a music genre — it's a way of living.** Singing is one expression of it; the whole of a surrendered life is the real thing (Romans 12:1). If students grasp that early, everything else — how they practice, how they treat their bandmates, how they carry themselves on and off a stage — flows out of it. The Fall term builds that theology of worship; the Winter term turns it toward the character of a worshiper and a leader.

Each study lists the core passage(s), the big idea, three or four discussion questions, and — where it fits — a one-line tie to that week's musicianship or song. Leaders should feel free to go where the conversation goes; these are rails, not scripts.

---

## Fall Term — "What Is Worship?"

### Week 1 — Kickoff: What is worship, really?
**Passage:** Romans 12:1–2. **Big idea:** Worship is offering your whole self to God — "your true and proper worship" — not just what happens when the music plays.
**Discuss:** When you hear the word "worship," what's the first picture in your head? · What would it mean for practicing your instrument, or how you treat people, to be "worship"? · What does it mean to not be "conformed to the pattern of this world"?
**Tie-in:** Sets the theme for the whole group. Great night to also just get to know each other — names, instruments, why they came.

### Week 2 — Worship in spirit and truth
**Passage:** John 4:1–26 (Jesus and the woman at the well; focus vv. 19–24). **Big idea:** God isn't after a location or a style — He's after worshipers who come in spirit and in truth.
**Discuss:** The woman wanted to argue about *where* to worship — what "where/how" debates do we still have? · What might "in spirit and in truth" look like for us? · Why do you think Jesus says the Father is *seeking* worshipers?

### Week 3 — Made to worship
**Passage:** Genesis 1:26–31 and Psalm 8. **Big idea:** We're made in God's image to reflect His glory; worship is us doing what we were built for.
**Discuss:** Psalm 8 asks "what is mankind that you are mindful of them?" — how does it feel that God is mindful of you? · If we're made to reflect God, what are we reflecting when we're *not* worshiping Him? · Where do you feel most "alive" — and could that be a clue to how you're wired to worship?

### Week 4 — The heart behind the song
**Passages:** 1 Samuel 16:1–13 (God looks at the heart) and Amos 5:21–24 (God rejects worship without heart/justice). **Big idea:** God cares more about the heart than the performance. He'll take an honest heart over a flawless one every time.
**Discuss:** God tells Samuel, "people look at the outward appearance, but the Lord looks at the heart" — how does that change how you think about being on a stage? · Amos is shocking: God says He *hates* their worship gatherings. Why? · Can you play a song perfectly and still not really worship?

### Week 5 — Why God is worth it (worth-ship)
**Passage:** Revelation 4–5 (the throne room; "Worthy is the Lamb"). **Big idea:** The old word "worth-ship" means declaring someone's worth. Worship is a response to who God actually is.
**Discuss:** What are the beings around the throne actually *doing*? · If worship is declaring worth, what does the way you spend your time and money say you think is worth the most? · Why does heaven never seem to get bored of it?

### Week 6 — David: the wholehearted worshiper
**Passage:** 2 Samuel 6:12–23 (David dances before the Lord; Michal despises him). **Big idea:** Real worship can look undignified. David cared more about honoring God than about how he looked.
**Discuss:** Michal was embarrassed by David — have you ever held back in worship because of what people might think? · David says he'll become "even more undignified than this." What do you make of that? · Where's the line between authentic expression and just performing?

### Week 7 — Worship when you don't feel it
**Passage:** Psalm 42–43 ("Why, my soul, are you downcast?"). **Big idea:** Worship isn't only for the good days. Sometimes it's talking to your own soul until your heart catches up.
**Discuss:** The psalmist argues with himself — "put your hope in God." Is that fake, or is that faith? · How do you worship on a day you don't feel like it? · Why might God be honored *more* by worship that costs you something emotionally?

### Week 8 — The Bible's own songbook
**Passages:** Overview of the Psalms; read Psalm 100. **Big idea:** God gave His people 150 songs. They model honesty, range, and structure — the same things we'll learn about writing and arranging.
**Discuss:** What's different about singing words God *gave* us versus words we came up with? · Psalm 100 is all celebration — but the Psalms also include anger and grief. Why include all of it? · What would it look like to make your own "psalm" this week?
**Tie-in:** Pairs naturally with the musicianship topic on charts/arrangement — the Psalms have structure too.

### Week 9 — Worship costs something
**Passages:** 2 Samuel 24:18–25 ("I will not sacrifice… that costs me nothing") and Mark 12:41–44 (the widow's offering). **Big idea:** Worship that costs nothing isn't worth much. God notices what it costs you, not what it's worth to everyone else.
**Discuss:** David refuses free stuff for his offering — why? · The widow gave two small coins; Jesus says she gave the most. How does God do the math differently than we do? · What does worship "cost" a musician — time, ego, comfort?

### Week 10 — Servant, not star
**Passages:** Philippians 2:1–11 (Christ's humility) and Colossians 3:23. **Big idea:** The musician on a worship team is a servant, not a star. We play for an audience of One and in service of the room.
**Discuss:** Jesus "made himself nothing" — what does that model for someone with a gift and a microphone? · What's the difference between using your gift to serve and using the platform to be seen? · "Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord" — how does that raise the bar on how we practice?

### Week 11 — Gratitude
**Passages:** Psalm 103 and 1 Thessalonians 5:16–18. **Big idea:** Thanksgiving is the native language of worship. "Give thanks in all circumstances" — not *for* everything, but *in* everything.
**Discuss:** Psalm 103 lists what God has done — make your own list out loud. · Why is gratitude so hard to hold onto? · How could gratitude change the vibe of a rehearsal or a hard week? *(Fits the week before Thanksgiving.)*

*(Nov 22 — Thanksgiving break, no meeting.)*

### Week 12 — The first Christmas songs
**Passage:** Luke 1:46–55 (Mary's Magnificat); optionally the angels in Luke 2:13–14. **Big idea:** When God stepped into the world, people burst into song. Worship is the natural response to good news.
**Discuss:** Mary's song is bold — God "brought down rulers" and "lifted up the humble." Does that match your picture of a Christmas song? · Why do you think Scripture keeps breaking into song at the big moments? · What's your honest response to the Christmas story this year?
**Tie-in:** Kicks off the Christmas repertoire for the Showcase.

### Week 13 — Point away from yourself
**Passages:** Colossians 1:15–20 and John 3:30 ("He must become greater; I must become less"). **Big idea:** A worship leader's whole job is to point past themselves to Jesus. The best leaders make you forget about the leader.
**Discuss:** John the Baptist was famous, and he said he wanted to *decrease* — why? · How do you lead people somewhere without making it about you? · What does it look like to "point to Jesus" with an instrument, not just words?

### Week 14 — Showcase & testimony
**Passage:** Light — Psalm 96:1–3 ("Sing to the Lord a new song… declare his glory among the nations"). **Big idea:** Celebrate the term. Play the songs. Share what God's been doing.
**Format:** Shorter study, longer celebration. Invite each student to say one thing they've learned — musically or spiritually. Then play. *(This is Showcase night — see the schedule.)*

## Winter Term — "The Worshiping Life"

### Week 15 — Reset: the worshiping life
**Passage:** Colossians 3:1–17 (set your hearts on things above; "whatever you do… do it all in the name of the Lord Jesus"). **Big idea:** A fresh start on the year. Worship is an orientation of the whole life, and it shows up in how we treat each other.
**Discuss:** Paul lists things to "put off" and "put on" — which one is God putting a finger on for you this year? · What's one goal — musical or spiritual — you want to set for this term?

### Week 16 — The full range of worship
**Passages:** Psalm 13 (lament) and Psalm 150 (pure praise). **Big idea:** Worship holds the whole range of human emotion. God can handle your honesty. "How long, Lord?" is in the songbook too.
**Discuss:** Psalm 13 starts angry and ends trusting — what changed? · Why is it healthy that the Bible's worship book includes complaints? · Which is easier for you to bring to God: praise or pain?

### Week 17 — Young and setting the example
**Passage:** 1 Timothy 4:12 ("Don't let anyone look down on you because you are young, but set an example…"). **Big idea:** Character on stage starts with character off stage. Who you are in private is who you really are.
**Discuss:** Paul names five areas: speech, conduct, love, faith, purity. Which is hardest right now? · Why would being young make people underestimate you — and how do you answer that? · Does what you post/say/do off stage matter to how you lead on it?

### Week 18 — One body, many parts
**Passage:** 1 Corinthians 12:12–27 (many parts, one body). **Big idea:** A band is a living picture of the church — different roles, one sound, nobody unnecessary.
**Discuss:** Paul says the parts that seem weaker are indispensable — how is that true in a band? · When have you wanted to be a different "part" than you are? · How do you celebrate a bandmate's moment instead of competing with it?
**Tie-in:** Pairs with the musicianship topic on being a bandmate and the rhythm-section relationship.

### Week 19 — Chasing God's presence
**Passages:** Exodus 33:12–23 ("If your Presence does not go with us…") and Psalm 84. **Big idea:** The goal was never the music. The goal is God Himself. Moses would rather have God's presence than the promised land.
**Discuss:** Moses turns down everything unless God comes too — do you want the gift or the Giver? · Psalm 84: "Better is one day in your courts than a thousand elsewhere." Do you believe that? · How do you keep the *music* from becoming the point?

### Week 20 — Use what you've been given
**Passages:** Matthew 25:14–30 (the talents) and 1 Peter 4:10–11. **Big idea:** God gives gifts to be invested, not buried. Using your gift *is* stewardship and worship.
**Discuss:** The servant who buried his talent was afraid — what fears keep us from using our gifts? · "Each of you should use whatever gift you have received to serve others" — who benefits when you develop your gift? · What's one way you could "invest" your musical gift this month?

### Week 21 — Worship as witness
**Passages:** Psalm 40:1–3 ("He put a new song in my mouth… many will see and fear") and Matthew 5:14–16. **Big idea:** A changed life set to music makes people curious about God. Worship spills outward.
**Discuss:** In Psalm 40 the *result* of the new song is that others come to trust God — have you ever been drawn to God by someone else's joy? · "Let your light shine before others" — how is playing music a kind of light? · Where's your "stage" outside of church?

### Week 22 — The one thing
**Passage:** Psalm 27:4 ("One thing I ask… to gaze on the beauty of the Lord"). **Big idea:** Commissioning. Under all the skills and gear, the lifelong aim is simply to know and enjoy God. Look ahead to spring.
**Format:** Reflect on the two terms, set individual next steps (a team to serve on, a skill to grow, a habit to keep), and pray over each student. Celebrate.

## Notes for leaders
- **Keep it discussion, not lecture.** The questions matter more than getting through the passage.
- **Model honesty.** Leaders sharing a real struggle unlocks the room faster than anything.
- **Connect it forward.** Where a study ties to the night's musicianship topic or song, name it out loud — students remember the theology when it's attached to their hands.
- **Alternate/backup studies** if you need them: Psalm 63, Isaiah 6:1–8 (Isaiah's vision & "here am I, send me"), Hebrews 12:28–29, John 12:1–8 (Mary's extravagant worship), 1 Chronicles 16, Nehemiah 8.
